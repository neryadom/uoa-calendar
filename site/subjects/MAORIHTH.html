Stage I

MAORIHTH 21H - Introduction to Biology (12 pts)üè≥

An introduction to the structure, function and processes of the human body at cellular and tissue levels. Special emphasis on the four primary tissues including membrane transport, muscle types and function, blood and the immune response, and basic neurobiology. Provides foundational knowledge of development post fertilisation and the anatomy and physiology of selected mammalian organ systems. Exposes students to the laboratory environment, particularly microscopy and dissection.

MAORIHTH 22H - Introduction to Anatomy and Physiology (12 pts)üè≥

Introduction to human biology through a study of the structure and function of mammalian organ systems. Topics of focus include: skin and derivatives, digestive, nervous, reproductive and urinary, bone, endocrine and circulatory systems. This course also exposes students to learning in a laboratory environment with a strong focus on microscopy and dissection.

MAORIHTH 23H - Introduction to Chemistry 1 (12 pts)üè≥

An overview of general chemistry principles with an emphasis on the language of chemistry and the use of mathematics to determine answers to chemical problems. An emphasis is placed on the atomic scale of matter so that students are able to describe the macroscopic world using a molecular perspective and relate chemical structures to functions. Laboratory work includes techniques of simple qualitative and quantitative measurements.

MAORIHTH 24H - Introduction to Chemistry 2 (12 pts)üè≥

Provides an overview of systematic organic chemistry illustrating the diversity and reactivity of organic compounds, including reaction mechanisms and application of chemical kinetics. Spectroscopic techniques will be discussed, including in relation to structure determination. A quantitative study of proton transfer reactions will allow for understanding of control of pH.

MAORIHTH 25H - Introduction to Population Health 1 (12 pts)üè≥

Introduction to key concepts and foundational knowledge in population health. Concepts include models for conceptualising health, the aggregate health of groups, social determinants of health, social gradients in health outcomes, and health inequalities and inequities, and foundational understanding of health care systems.

MAORIHTH 26H - Introduction to Population Health 2 (12 pts)üè≥

Explores patterns and distributions in health events, causal effects on health, and strategies for addressing health inequalities and inequities at a population level. Exposure to a foundational overview of epidemiology and population health concepts and relevant skills, including understanding and measuring the distribution of disease and illness in well-defined populations, will also be provided.

MAORIHTH 27H - Academic and Professional Development in MƒÅori and (12 pts)üè≥

Pacific Health 1 Presents study and academic writing skills essential for successful transition from secondary education or community contexts into tertiary study. Content focuses on a practical application of Population Health and MƒÅori and Pacific health workforce development, while engaging students in their professional practice, cultural growth and leadership and communication.

MAORIHTH 28H - Academic and Professional Development in MƒÅori and (12 pts)üè≥

Pacific Health 2 Provides study and academic skills necessary for transition from foundation study to first year bachelor level study. Content areas focus on MƒÅori and Pacific relevant examples of population health topics including: health status, determinants of health, barriers to access and quality of care and health interventions targeted at MƒÅori and Pacific populations.

MAORIHTH 29H - Introduction to Mathematics (12 pts)üè≥

Provides foundation skills in mathematics and develops mathematical competence. Topics covered include measurement, notation, functions, equations, exponential growth/decay, logarithms and statistics. Examples used in the course will revolve around applications of mathematics in the health sciences.

MAORIHTH 30H - Introduction to Health Psychology (12 pts)üè≥

Introduction to key concepts and foundational knowledge in health psychology. Concepts include models and theories of behaviour change and development, including the relationship between major biological, cognitive and social- emotional processes. Broader social science approaches to behaviour, health and development across the lifespan will also be explored, as well as the application of health psychology for those wishing to pursue a career in health.

MAORIHTH 31H - Introduction to Physics (12 pts)üè≥

An introduction to physics relevant to health studies, including examples and illustrations that revolve around human physiology. Topics include mechanics, optics, waves, thermal physics, radiation and electricity.

Stage II

MAORIHTH 201 - Introduction to MƒÅori Health (15 pts)üè≥

MƒÅori society, culture and values are explored. Historical processes are reviewed within the context of the Treaty of Waitangi. The course will examine how these factors underpin the basic determinants of health and shape contemporary MƒÅori health status in Aotearoa. Different approaches to improving MƒÅori health and reducing inequalities will be critically examined.

Prerequisite: POPLHLTH 111

MƒÅori health knowledge is used to develop effective public health practice for MƒÅori contexts. Areas of focus include critical thinking, reflective practice, advocacy and the application of Kaupapa MƒÅori principles.

MAORIHTH 701 - Foundations of MƒÅori Health (15 pts)üè≥

Provides an overview of the many dimensions of MƒÅori Health. It examines the historical and contemporary determinants of MƒÅori health status, and outlines strategies for improving MƒÅori health in the context of the Treaty of Waitangi, and reducing health inequalities.

Restriction: MAORIHTH 301

MAORIHTH 705 - MƒÅori Health Promotion and Early Intervention (15 pts)üè≥

Discusses the importance of health promotion and early intervention for MƒÅori. Models of health promotion used by different MƒÅori providers will be presented as well as assisting students to design and implement health promotion and interventions which are likely to be effective for MƒÅori individuals, families, and communities.

MAORIHTH 706 - MƒÅori Health: Policy and Practice (15 pts)üè≥

Critically examines public health policy and practice in Aotearoa/New Zealand with respect to MƒÅori health and equity. Provides insights into the application of Kaupapa MƒÅori principles in different areas of public health practice to advance MƒÅori health.

Prerequisite: MAORIHTH 301 or 701

MAORIHTH 707 - Practicum in MƒÅori Health (15 pts)üè≥

Provides the opportunity to develop social assessment and critical analysis skills through the documentation of an approved practicum. Students will be expected to be able to use and demonstrate knowledge of different MƒÅori views, concepts and frameworks. Each student will have supervision and practicum developed appropriate to their learning interests.

MAORIHTH 709 - Transformational Research for MƒÅori Health (15 pts)üè≥

Provides a critical analysis of research and research processes with regard to their potential to colonise or liberate. Drawing on Kaupapa MƒÅori Theory, the course examines how research can be undertaken in ways that are safe for MƒÅori and that contribute to positive MƒÅori development.

Prerequisite: MAORIHTH 710

MAORIHTH 710 - Kaupapa MƒÅori Theory (15 pts)üè≥

Kaupapa MƒÅori Theory (KMT) underpins a range of approaches employed to ensure policy, research and intervention processes emphasise MƒÅori ways of knowing and being and work to prevent the further marginalisation of MƒÅori. Students learn about the development of KMT and its use in the context of MƒÅori health and development, and will experience and learn from a range of initiatives and projects that have KMT at their core.

Prerequisite: MAORIHTH 301 or 701

Restriction: MAORIHTH 702

MAORIHTH 711 - Special Topic: MƒÅori Quantitative Methods (15 pts)üè≥

Provides students with an understanding of how to apply a Kaupapa MƒÅori Research (KMR) approach to quantitative research methods (study design, analysis and dissemination) in the health sciences. It will expose students to a range of analytic and practical tools that can be drawn on in the design and conduct of quantitative research with MƒÅori.

Prerequisite: MAORIHTH 301 or 701
